## Conservative Bias Rationale

### Why Lower Than Recent Trends?

**Property Growth (30-year horizon):**
- Recent 10-year CAGR: 9.2% (ČSÚ)
- Recommended: 3.0-4.0% (56-67% reduction)

**Justification:**
1. **Current overvaluation** - ČNB warned prices 10-15% above fundamentals in 2018; worse now
2. **Mean reversion** - Academic research shows fast reversion to equilibrium after booms
3. **International benchmarks** - European 3.5%, global long-term real returns 0-1%
4. **User protection** - Better to underestimate than overestimate over 30 years
5. **Uncertainty modeling** - Monte Carlo mode captures boom scenarios (P90 ~7%)

**Rent Growth (30-year horizon):**
- Recent 10-year CAGR: 3.8% (OECD)
- Recommended: 3.0-3.5% (79-92% of historical)

**Justification:**
1. **Income constraint** - Rent can't sustainably exceed wage growth
2. **2024 anomaly** - +17% YoY was post-COVID catch-up, normalizing to 3.1%
3. **Stable anchor** - Rent growth more stable than property prices
4. **Conservative estimate** - Close to inflation (2.5%) + modest premium

## Formula Documentation

### Property Growth Formula
```
Nominal property growth = Inflation + Real appreciation + City premium

Where:
- Inflation: 2.5% (ČNB forecast)
- Real appreciation: 0-1.5% (academic research)
- City premium:
  * Praha/Brno: 1.0-1.5% (tier 1 cities)
  * Large regional: 0.5-1.0% (tier 2)
  * Smaller cities: 0.0-0.5% (tier 3)

Result:
- Praha/Brno: 2.5% + 1.0% + 0.5% = 4.0%
- Large regional: 2.5% + 0.5% + 0.5% = 3.5%
- Smaller cities: 2.5% + 0.0% + 0.5% = 3.0%
```

### Rent Growth Formula
```
Nominal rent growth = Inflation + Real wage growth + Demand factor

Where:
- Inflation: 2.5%
- Real wage growth: 0-0.5% (post-convergence)
- Demand factor:
  * Praha/Brno: 0.5% (tight markets)
  * Other cities: 0.0% (balanced)

Result:
- Praha/Brno: 2.5% + 0.5% + 0.5% = 3.5%
- Other cities: 2.5% + 0.5% + 0.0% = 3.0%
```
